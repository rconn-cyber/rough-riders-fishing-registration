Tournament Overview

The Rough Riders 31st Annual Charity Fishing Tournament is an Offshore and Inshore fishing event benefiting the 1st U.S. Volunteer Cavalry Regiment – Rough Riders, Inc. By registering, all participants agree to comply with these Rules and accept all Tournament Committee decisions as final. This is a skill-based competition.

📅 Lines In: 6:00 AM · Fishing Ends: 4:00 PM · Offshore weigh-in must be acknowledged "in line" at the Fusion Hotel by 4:00 PM

⚓ Tournament Boundaries: Gulf of America — New Pass to the north tip of Anclote Island · Inland waters — Oldsmar to New Pass

Divisions & Scoring

🐟 Inshore Division Photo · Length

  • Species: Snook and Redfish
  • Scoring: Total length of the two largest fish — one of each species
  • Format: Catch, photograph, and release only — no weigh-in
  • Submissions must be made via the official app within 1 hour
  • Provided playing card visible as identifier required in photo
  • Entire fish visible on official measuring device; mouth closed, tail properly positioned
  • Measurements recorded to the nearest ¼ inch

🎣 Offshore Division Physical Weigh-In

  • Species: Red Grouper and American Red Snapper
  • Entry Limit: Two (2) Grouper and two (2) Red Snapper per boat
  • Scoring: Aggregate weight of all four fish
  • Weigh-In: Fusion Hotel waterside — 290 107th Ave N, Treasure Island
  • Scales open 1:00 PM – close 4:00 PM on Saturday, June 20, 2026
  • Fish must be fresh and unaltered; all foreign objects (hooks, line) removed prior to weigh-in
  • Must be acknowledged in line by tournament staff by 4:00 PM — no late arrivals accepted

🦈 Tarpon Division Release · Count

  • Minimum size: 48 inches — lip to fork (estimated). Micro tarpon not eligible.
  • Scoring: Each valid, approved release counts as one (1)
  • Format: Catch, photograph, and release only — no weigh-in permitted
  • Maximum 8 rods per boat; all anglers must fish from the same boat
  • Leader in hand + provided playing card visible in photo required
  • Up to 4 anglers may register releases per boat
  • Tie breaker: Team to release the final scoring fish wins (based on photo receipt time)

Catch & Release Rules

Tackle Fishing Methods

  • Rod and reel only — manually operated tackle
  • Artificial lures and/or natural bait permitted
  • Leaders may not exceed 10 feet — no tackle modification during the fight
  • Rod holders permitted; angler must remove rod immediately upon strike
  • Harnesses and rod belts permitted
  • No electric reels, spearfishing, snagging, or gigging
  • Assistance is prohibited except for safety reasons

Tarpon Release Rules

  • Fish must remain completely in the water at all times — no lifting into boats or onto land
  • A release is valid when the fish is under control boatside and the leader is in hand
  • Fish must swim away under its own power
  • Dead or improperly handled fish will not be scored
  • Multiple fish may be fought simultaneously; rods may be passed between registered anglers
  • Only registered anglers may touch the rod, reel, or line (except leader handling for release)

Photo Photo Submissions

  • All submissions must be made via the official app — stored or external photos not accepted
  • Photos must be clear, unaltered, and unobstructed — one fish per photo
  • Releases must be submitted within 1 hour via the app
  • Anglers are responsible for confirming successful submission
  • Missing elements will result in rejection unless additional evidence is approved

⚠️ Photos must be taken within the official app. Stored or external photos are not accepted under any circumstances.

Awards, Protests & Eligibility

Awards Prizes & Tie Breakers

  • Prizes awarded per division
  • Tie Breaker: Earliest valid submission (photo divisions); earliest weigh-in time (offshore)
  • Tournament Committee decision is final in all tie scenarios

Protests Protest Procedure

  • Must be submitted in writing by 7:00 PM, Saturday, June 20, 2026
  • Must include a $200 deposit
  • Decisions made by the Rough Rider Fishing Tournament Committee and are final
  • Photo compliance decisions are final and non-appealable
  • Prize payouts will not be delayed pending protests

Eligibility Eligibility Requirements

  • Participation limited to anglers fishing from a pre-registered tournament boat
  • All entry fees must be paid prior to the start of the tournament
  • All anglers must possess valid fishing licenses as required by law
  • Boats may only compete in the division(s) for which they are registered
  • All fishing must comply with FWC and all applicable local, state, and federal regulations

⚠️ Disqualification grounds include: Rule violations · Fraudulent submissions · Legal violations · Unsportsmanlike conduct. All disqualification decisions are final.

Waiver Liability Waiver

Participants fish at their own risk. The 1st U.S. Volunteer Cavalry Regiment – Rough Riders, Inc., and all affiliated parties assume no liability for injury, loss, or damage, including negligence, to the fullest extent permitted by Florida law.
